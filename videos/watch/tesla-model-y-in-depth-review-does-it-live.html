The long-awaited Tesla Model Y has now arrived, adding trendy SUV-styling to much of the same running gear and tech to be found in the Tesla Model 3. Choice in this area of the market is growing rapidly, with the Volkswagen ID.4, Ford Mustang Mach-E, Skoda Enyaq iV, Audi Q4 e-tron and Hyundai Ioniq 5 all providing extremely competent competition. The Model Y offers a range of 298-315 miles, a minimalist interior, practicality and plenty of power, plus the cachet of the Tesla badge. Does it still stand out in an increasingly large crowd? Nicola finds out.
