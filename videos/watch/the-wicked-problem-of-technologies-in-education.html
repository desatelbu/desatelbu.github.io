What would happen if we stopped pretending that technologies were solutions to teaching or learning?

Digital technologies are often talked about as solutions - artefacts and practices - that can be simply parachuted from one classroom to the next to solve problems or improve outcomes. However, these are rarely achieved without considerable skill and orchestration by educators who can adapt to the ripples of complexity caused by inserting new technologies and practices. Every day, in schools and in higher education, we are faced with the ‚Äòwicked problem‚Äô of bringing together the potentiality of technology with the complexity of education, not least the diversity of students, teachers, schools and policy.
