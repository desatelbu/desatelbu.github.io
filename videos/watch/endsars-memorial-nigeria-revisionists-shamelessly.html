It has now been a year since the Lekki massacre of young ENDSARS protesters by the Nigerian government. Prior to the killings and amidst the coronavirus pandemic that was ravaging at the time, Nigerians took to the streets to protest police brutality and the structural forces that surrounds it.
After what appeared to be a national effort to reckon with systemic police brutality how have we fared? Reflecting on the anniversary of the LekkiToll Gate massacre Dele Farotimi a legal practitioner and activist shares with SaharaTV the the injustices that were animated and the attempts by revisionists to distorts the truth.
