The International Atomic Energy Agency has struck a deal with Iran on replacing cameras at the Karaj complex. The United Nations nuclear watchdog said it has struck an agreement with Iran to replace its surveillance cameras at the centrifuge-parts workshop. Iran had removed them after an apparent sabotage attack in June. The UN Agency says the new cameras would be installed in the coming days.

Guest: Professor Seyed Marandi Iranian academic and Chair of North American Studies.
