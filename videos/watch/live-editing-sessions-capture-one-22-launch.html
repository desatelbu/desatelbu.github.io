1) Auto-Rotate.
Don‚Äôt confuse with auto-keystone, this is a very different thing.
While AI-based, it tends to be confused sometimes with vertical lines, prioritising those over obvious horizons.
Where the rotation is purely around the horizon level, sometimes manual is still a safer bet - but the clear advantage here is it can deliver a different result to each of a much bigger batch as a selection.

2) Pano Stitching.
For those looking for a quick hand-held stitch, the results are surprisingly good.
The output of the panorama as a DNG raw is the killer app here - retaining full editing capabilities.
Don‚Äôt over-shoot the sources (too many frames, too much overlap, causes issues with all stitching software).
Overall, the more care taken to getting good input content, the better the end result (especially with nodal rails, etc).
Certain projections (panini) are better for architecture than others (spherical), but take longer to process. Experiment with them all as they‚Äôre cached once generated.
De-ghosting would be helpful, saving potential "heals" after the stitch and the inevitable hunt to find any joins.
I believe this has some work to go with it, but that‚Äôs likely the intention - for now, it does 90% of scenarios pretty well.

3) HDR Merge.
My least favourite (mostly as a style, not the tool itself).
It depends on what camera you‚Äôre coming from, but if you can already shoot 15, 16, 18 stops of dynamic range (as we can on a Phase One IQ4), you‚Äôll often find a more natural result using the (very good) HDR sliders on a single exposure.
The lack of ghost removal / fusion settings is a challenge - as it‚Äôs possible to see artefacts in certain (mainly moving) situations - but then HDR isn‚Äôt great on moving subjects anyway.
Auto-Adjust delivers quite a cartoony-output as a finished result, given the slider manipulation that‚Äôs done for you. Some may like that, I think it looks too "typical HDR".
Auto-Align is great if you have any movement between the frames. If you‚Äôre steady on a tripod, however, disable this.
